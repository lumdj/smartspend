# SmartSpend — User Guide

## Getting Started

### Creating Your Profile

When you first open SmartSpend, you'll go through a short onboarding flow. This takes about 2 minutes and is the most important step — SmartSpend uses your profile to personalize every insight, nudge, and recommendation it gives you.

You'll be asked about:
- Your age and occupation
- Where your income comes from and roughly how much
- How long you've had a credit card and what your limit is
- Your main financial goal right now
- Your biggest spending weakness (be honest — the app uses this)
- How stressed you feel about money
- Whether you usually pay your full balance each month
- When your credit card statement closes (optional, but improves accuracy)

None of this is shared with anyone. It stays in the app and is only used to make your coaching more relevant.

> **Tip:** You can update any of this later in Settings. If your situation changes — new job, new card, feeling less stressed — update your profile and the AI adjusts.

## The Dashboard

The dashboard is your home screen. It answers three questions at a glance:

**How am I doing this month?**
Your Financial Health Score (0–100) sits at the top. Below it, you'll see your total spend for the current billing cycle vs. your income, and your credit utilization indicator.

**Do I need to do anything?**
Active nudges and alerts appear here — spending spikes, credit limit warnings, goal opportunities. Tap any nudge to see the full explanation. Dismiss it when you're done.

**How are my goals going?**
Your active goals (up to 3) show as progress bars with the current amount and target. Tap a goal to see its full history and make a manual deposit.

### The Health Score

Your score is calculated from three things:
- Credit utilization (how much of your limit you're using)
- Spending mix (what percentage is essential vs. discretionary)
- Income ratio (how your total spend compares to your income)

A score above 75 is healthy. Below 50 means there's something that needs attention — tap the score to see what's pulling it down.

## Transactions

The transactions page shows every purchase categorized and explained.

### Categories

SmartSpend automatically classifies every transaction as:
- **Essential** — rent, groceries, utilities, transportation, healthcare
- **Semi-essential** — phone bill, gym, streaming subscriptions
- **Discretionary** — dining out, shopping, entertainment, coffee

### Correcting a Category

If SmartSpend gets a merchant wrong — Walmart categorized as electronics instead of groceries, for example — tap the transaction and select the correct category. SmartSpend remembers your correction for that merchant going forward.

### Nudges on Transactions

Some transactions will have an AI nudge attached — a brief coaching note about why that purchase matters in the context of your goals or credit situation. These only appear when something is worth flagging, not on every purchase.

### Financial Terms

Any financial term in blue is tap-to-expand. Tap it to see a plain-language explanation specific to your situation. You won't need a glossary.

## Goals

### Creating a Goal

Tap **+ New Goal** from the dashboard or goals page. You'll set:
- A name (be specific — "Trip to Japan" beats "Travel")
- A type (travel, purchase, emergency fund, debt paydown, savings, custom)
- A target amount
- An optional target date
- An emoji to represent it
- An optional reason — why this goal matters to you

The reason field might feel optional, but it's not. Research consistently shows that people who articulate *why* they're saving reach their goals faster. SmartSpend references your reason in coaching nudges.

### Linking a Goal to a Spending Category

You can link a goal to a spending category — for example, link your "Trip to Japan" goal to dining. When you spend less on dining than your monthly average, SmartSpend automatically calculates how much you "saved" and can apply it toward your goal.

Auto-progress is an estimate, not a bank transfer. It shows you the connection between daily spending decisions and your goals.

### Manual Deposits

Tap a goal and select **Add Progress** to manually record a contribution. This is for when you've actually set money aside — transferred to a savings account, for example — and want the app to reflect it.

### Monthly Recap Deposits

At the end of each month, SmartSpend runs your monthly recap. After reviewing your summary, you'll see a simple allocation screen: "You came in under budget by $180 this month. Where do you want to put it?" Tap a goal and enter an amount. One screen, one minute.

## Monthly Recap

The monthly recap runs automatically at the end of each month, or you can access it anytime from the dashboard.

It shows:
- Your Financial Health Score for the month with a narrative explanation
- Total spend breakdown — essential vs. discretionary, vs. last month
- Your biggest category and whether it went up or down
- Any achievements you unlocked this month
- Action items for next month — specific, numbered, based on your actual data
- The goal allocation screen

The recap is the most important ritual in the app. It closes the loop on the month and gives you a clean slate for the next one.

## Achievements

Achievements are earned for real financial behavior — not for opening the app. You can't grind them. They unlock when you actually do something worth recognizing.

### Achievement Categories

- **Spending** — Under Budget, Dining Detective, Subscription Audit
- **Credit** — Credit Conscious (under 30% utilization), Debt Dodger (full balance paid)
- **Goals** — Dream Starter, Halfway There, Goal Crusher
- **Streaks** — Week Warrior, Monthly Regular
- **Milestones** — First Full Balance Month, First Goal Created

When you unlock an achievement, you'll see a celebration screen and an education card explaining the financial concept behind what you just did. The achievement means something — the card tells you why.

---

## Education Cards

Education cards appear when you hit a meaningful moment — your utilization crosses 50% for the first time, you complete your first goal, you pay your full balance for the first time.

Each card has:
- A title that names the concept
- A plain-language explanation written specifically for you
- One concrete action to take
- One number that makes it real

Cards are generated by AI in the context of your actual situation. The explanation a 19-year-old first-time cardholder gets for "credit utilization" is different from what a 32-year-old working professional gets.

You can review past cards anytime from your profile.

## Settings

### Updating Your Profile

Any field from onboarding can be updated in Settings → Edit Profile. If your income changes, your credit limit increases, or your stress level drops, update it. The AI coaching adjusts immediately.

### Billing Cycle

Settings → Billing Cycle. Enter the day of the month your statement closes. If you're not sure, check your card's app or your last statement. This makes your utilization tracking significantly more accurate.

### Reset Data

Settings → Reset Data clears all your transactions and progress. Your profile is preserved. Use this if you want to start fresh with new synthetic data or if you're handing the app to someone else to try.

---

## Privacy

SmartSpend does not connect to your bank. All transaction data is either synthetic (the demo data loaded when you first sign up) or manually imported. No real financial credentials are ever entered or stored.

Your profile data is stored securely and is only used to personalize your in-app experience.
