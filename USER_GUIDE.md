# SmartSpend — User Guide

## Getting Started

### Creating Your Profile

When you first open SmartSpend, you'll go through a short onboarding flow. This takes about 2 minutes and is the most important step — SmartSpend uses your profile to personalize every insight, nudge, and recommendation it gives you.

You'll be asked about:
- Your age and occupation
- Where your income comes from and roughly how much
- Your credit limit and how long you've had a credit card
- When your credit card statement closes (optional, but improves utilization accuracy)
- Your main financial goal right now
- Your biggest spending weakness
- How stressed you feel about money (1–5)
- Whether you usually pay your full balance each month

None of this is shared with anyone. It stays in the app and is only used to make your coaching more relevant.

> **Tip:** You can update any of this later in Settings. If your situation changes — new job, new card, stress level improves — update your profile and the AI adjusts immediately.

## The Dashboard

The dashboard answers three questions at a glance:

**How is my financial health trending?**
The health score chart sits at the top — a 0–100 score with a 6-month area chart and a directional arrow (↑ green for improving, ↓ red for declining). Below it are three stat cards: total spend this period, discretionary spend, and credit utilization.

**Do I need to do anything right now?**
Active nudges and alerts appear below the stats — spending spikes, credit limit warnings, education cards, and AI coaching messages. Tap any nudge to see the full explanation. Use 👍👎 to give feedback on whether it was useful.

**How are my goals going?**
Active goals show as progress bars at the bottom. Tap a goal to see its history and add progress.

### The Health Score

Scored 0–100 from three components:
- **Credit utilization** (40 pts) — full score under 30%, zero above 70%
- **Discretionary spend ratio** (30 pts) — full score under 40% of total
- **Income spend ratio** (30 pts) — full score under 60% of income

Alert penalties: -5 for each critical alert, -2 for each warning.

## Transactions

The transactions page shows every purchase categorized by priority.

### Priority Levels
- **Essential** — groceries, utilities, transportation, healthcare
- **Semi-essential** — phone bill, gym, streaming subscriptions
- **Discretionary** — dining, shopping, entertainment, coffee

### Correcting a Category
If SmartSpend miscategorizes a merchant — Walmart as electronics instead of groceries — tap the transaction and select the correct category. The correction is remembered for that merchant going forward.

### Filters
Filter by month, priority level, or search by merchant. Combine filters to drill into specific spending patterns.

## Goals

### Creating a Goal
Tap **+ New Goal** and set a name, type, target amount, and optionally a target date, emoji, and reason. The reason field matters — SmartSpend references it in coaching nudges to connect your spending decisions to something you actually care about.

You can have up to 3 active goals at once.

### Linking to a Category
Link a goal to a spending category (e.g. link your travel goal to dining). When your dining spend drops below your average, SmartSpend automatically credits the difference toward your goal.

### Adding Progress
Tap a goal and select **Add Progress** to manually record a contribution — for when you've actually transferred money to a savings account. Enter the amount and it updates your progress bar immediately.

### Goal Completion
When a goal hits 100%, it's automatically marked complete and a celebration nudge is queued. The completion also unlocks the **Goal Crusher** achievement.

## Monthly Recap

Access from the nav or it triggers automatically at month end.

The recap shows:
- Your Financial Health Score for the month with an AI narrative
- Spending breakdown — essential vs. discretionary, vs. last month
- Top spending categories
- Any achievements earned this month
- Three specific action items for next month
- The biggest financial risk this month

Use the month selector to browse past months.

## Learning

The Learning tab is your personal financial education library. Cards are generated by Claude specifically for your situation when you hit a milestone:

| Trigger | Card topic |
|---|---|
| Utilization crosses 50% | Credit utilization and score impact |
| Utilization crosses 70% | The danger zone — score damage timeline |
| First goal created | Psychology of savings goals |
| Goal reaches 50% | Momentum and commitment consistency |
| Goal completed | Compounding the savings habit |
| First full balance month | What you saved in interest |
| 4+ subscriptions detected | Subscription creep |
| Dining > 25% of income | Small purchase blindness |
| Spending > 90% of income | The buffer and emergency fund |
| Stress level = 5 | Financial anxiety and the avoidance cycle |

Each card has a plain-language explanation written for your experience level, one memorable number, and one concrete next step.

Tap any underlined term for an inline definition. Reading 5 cards unlocks the **Knowledge Seeker** achievement.

## Settings

### Updating Your Profile
Any onboarding field can be updated in Settings → Edit Profile. Changes take effect on the next AI call.

### Billing Cycle
Settings → Billing Cycle. Enter the day your statement closes (1–28). This makes utilization tracking more accurate — without it, SmartSpend falls back to calendar month.

### Reset Data
Settings → Reset (or tap Reset in the nav bar). Clears all transactions and generated data. Your profile is preserved. Use this to start fresh or hand the app to someone else to try.

## Demo Control Panel

Navigate directly to `/demo` in the browser (not linked in nav).

Use it to:
- **Load a persona** — switches transaction history to Alex (stressed undergrad), Jordan (recent grad), or Taylor (disciplined professional)
- **Spike spending** — inflates dining or shopping to trigger alerts
- **Trigger education cards** — manually fires any of the 12 card triggers to populate the Learning tab
- **Reset** — wipes transaction data and reloads with a fresh persona

## Privacy

SmartSpend does not connect to your real bank. All transaction data is synthetic (generated on first login) or manually imported. No real financial credentials are ever entered or stored.

Your profile data is stored in the app's database and is only used to personalize your experience.
